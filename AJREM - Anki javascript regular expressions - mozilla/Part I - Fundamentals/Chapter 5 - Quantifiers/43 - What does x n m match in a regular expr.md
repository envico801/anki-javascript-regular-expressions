========== Question ==========  

### What does `x{n,m}` match in a regular expression?  

========== Answer ==========  

Matches between 'n' and 'm' occurrences of 'x'.

**Example**: `a{2,4}` matches "aa", "aaa", and "aaaa", but not "a" or "aaaaa".
