Q: What does `x+` match in a regular expression pattern?  
A: `x+` matches the preceding item "x" 1 or more times. It is equivalent to `{1,}`.  
For example, `/a+/` matches the "a" in "candy" and all the "a"'s in "caaaaaaandy".
